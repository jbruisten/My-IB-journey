The Course Journey: Module Reflections

Module 1

The Paradigm Shift

Module 1 marked the realization that the IB is not merely a curriculum but a philosophy of inquiry. I shifted from seeing teaching as content delivery to seeing it as the cultivation of inquiring, knowledgeable, and caring young people.

Module 2

Unit Planning & Inclusive Design

Focused on developing an "Acids and Bases" unit that prioritizes accessibility. By using dyslexia-friendly fonts like Bahnschrift and integrating the Dutch RTTI system, I explored how to bridge local assessment literacy with global IB standards.

Module 3

Assessment & The tightrope of SEN

I examined the Internal Assessment (IA) marking process and the unique challenges faced by students with learning disabilities. I realized that scaffolding is the "net" that allows students to walk the tightrope of independent scientific inquiry without falling.

Module 4

Collaboration & Trauma-Sensitivity

Investigated the dynamics of Professional Learning Communities (PLCs). I explored Haim Omer's "New Authority" and the role of relational trust in trauma-sensitive teaching, emphasizing that consistency across a team is the foundation of student safety.

Module 2: Acids and Bases Unit Planning

DP Unit Planner: Acids and Bases

Sciences: Chemistry DP (SL/HL)

View Unit Planner Document
Planner SectionContent Details
Unit Description
"This unit explores the nature of acids and bases. The definitions, formation, reactions, and applications. Students will investigate how different scientific models can explain acid-base behaviour and how acidity is measured. Furthermore, students will experience that chemical knowledge is constructed through experimentation, data analysis and evaluation of evidence."
Transfer Goals
By the end of this unit, students will be able to: - Apply acid-base concepts and models to unfamiliar chemical systems, using evidence and reasoning for explaining reactions, predicting outcomes and justifying conclusions without teacher guidance. - Interpret and evaluate acidity-related data (such as pH measurements, titration curves, or indicator responses) to make informed decisions about chemical behaviour in real-world contexts. - Use chemical understanding to solve authentic problems, involving acids and bases, considering limitations of models, uncertainty in measurements, and the appropriateness of different analytical methods.
Guiding Questions
  • How do chemists define acids and bases and why are there multiple models needed to explain their behaviour? (Arrhenius, Brønsted-Lowry, Lewis)
  • How can acidity be measured, interpreted, and compared using different methods and technologies? (pH scale, indicators, probes)
  • How can acid-base chemistry be applied to unfamiliar, real-world situations? (transfer, problem-solving, authentic contexts)

Module 3: Scaffolding and Assessment

Thematic PostOriginal Reflection Content
The "Tightrope" Reflection
"For students with learning disabilities, the Zone of Proximal Development is like walking on a tightrope. Scaffolding is the net that ensures they don't fall while trying to reach higher levels of independent thinking. I initially expected the IB to be mainly a structured curriculum, but I understanding that it is much more focused on inquiry and reflection. This differs from more traditional systems like the Dutch 'Profielwerkstuk'. In the lab, we must ensure the 'net' is strong enough for them to feel safe to fail."
IA marking insights
"Through the marking exercises, I recognized that IB assessment is a dialogue between the student’s exploration and the criteria. It’s not just about the final answer, but about the 'personal engagement' and the justification of the research design. This reinforces the need for qualitative feedback during the formative stages."

Relational Trust Reflection
"Module 4 highlighted how important collaboration and trust are, but also how difficult they can be in practice. I recognised that strong relationships don’t automatically lead to effective collaboration—teams can feel safe but still avoid meaningful change. For me, it reinforced that reflection should not stay individual, but needs to be part of honest, sometimes uncomfortable dialogue within a team to really improve practice."
