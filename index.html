The Foundation

IB Mission Statement

"The International Baccalaureate aims to develop inquiring, knowledgeable and caring young people who help to create a better and more peaceful world through intercultural understanding and respect... These programmes encourage students across the world to become active, compassionate and lifelong learners who understand that other people, with their differences, can also be right."

My Personal Vision

"I see the IB philosophy as a system that uses reflection, relationships and inquiry to pursue meaningful learning."

A Reciprocal Cycle of Learning

How daily practice influences students, and how their growth impacts the teacher.

Daily Teacher Practice

Intentional Scaffolding

Identifying the "tightrope" of the student's Zone of Proximal Development, providing templates and digital tools (Excel/ColorGrab) to lower barriers for students with learning disabilities.

Student Influence

Independent Inquiry

Students move from mere task completion to asking deep "Why" questions, gaining the autonomy to explore scientific models through their own data analysis.

Daily Teacher Practice

Relational Trust

Applying trauma-sensitive strategies (New Authority) to build a consistent, safe environment where failure is treated as a necessary step in the scientific process.

Student Influence

Emotional Resilience

Students share their anomalies and uncertainties with honesty rather than fear, building the resilience required for academic and personal growth.

How Students Impact the Teacher

"When students begin to reflect honestly, they challenge my own assumptions. Their growth teaches me to be a more responsive practitioner. I learn that my routine is not the curriculum—the relationship is. Their successes refine my scaffolding, and their challenges keep my own reflective practice alive."

The Course Journey

01

Paradigm Shift: The IB Foundations

Reflection

"Module 1 was my first real introduction to the IB. I initially expected a structured curriculum, but I came to understand it is focused on inquiry and the whole student. This challenged me as it differs from outcome-driven systems like the Dutch 'Profielwerkstuk'. It taught me to move beyond task-guiding to supporting independent, reflective learners."

02

Inquiry-Led Unit Planning

Development of the Acids & Bases DP Unit. Focused on integrating technological tools while maintaining accessibility through dyslexia-friendly typography (Bahnschrift).

Key Learning:

Teaching ethical AI usage as a 'supportive assistant' rather than a ghostwriter—strengthening student voice through technological literacy.

03

Assessment & Inclusive Scaffolding

IA Marking & Standards

Learning to value depth in research design over layout. Recognizing the IB requirement for a high degree of student personal engagement and reflection.

"For students with learning disabilities, the Zone of Proximal Development is like walking on a tightrope. Scaffolding is the net that ensures they don't fall while trying to reach higher levels of independent thinking."

04

The Collaborative Community

Week 1: Culture & Trust (Fenwick)
Week 2: Inquiry Culture & Viva Voce
Week 3: Socratic Reflection in Science
Week 4: PLC Case Study (New Authority)

Final Reflection on Collaboration

"Module 4 highlighted that strong relationships don’t automatically lead to effective collaboration—teams can feel safe but still avoid meaningful change. Reflection should not stay individual, but needs to be part of honest, sometimes uncomfortable dialogue within a team to really improve practice."
