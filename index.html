Module 2: DP Unit Planner - Acids and Bases

Section Details & Implementation
Unit Description This unit explores the nature of acids and bases, their formation, reactions, and applications. Students investigate scientific models and experience how chemical knowledge is constructed through experimentation and evaluation of evidence.
Inclusive Design Dyslexia Friendly: All materials, including the unit plan itself, use the Bahnschrift font to improve readability for students with learning disabilities.
Formative Assessment Integration of the Dutch RTTI system (Reproduction, Training 1, Training 2, Insight) to scaffold cognitive levels in Paper 1 style assessments.
Digital Literacy Use of analytical tools such as ColorGrab for pH indicator analysis and Excel for processing large datasets, teaching ethical AI usage as a 'supportive assistant'.
TOK Integration Connections to "Knowledge and the Knower" by examining how chemical models are constructed and revised based on evidence.

Module 3: Assessment & Inclusive Scaffolding

Focus Area Critical Reflection
IA Marking Exercise Shifted from Dutch curriculum standards to IB criteria. Identified that IB values personal engagement and depth of research design over just correct data. Marked sample IAs to align with global moderation standards.
SEN Scaffolding Implemented "Zone of Proximal Development" theory specifically for chemistry labs. Provided pre-structured Excel sheets and lab-procedure flowcharts to lower cognitive load for students with processing difficulties.

Module 4: PLC & Trauma-Sensitive Teaching

Week/Component Full Reflection Text
Collaborative Learning Strong relationships don’t automatically lead to effective collaboration. Teams can feel safe but still avoid meaningful change. Reflection must move from individual thought to honest, uncomfortable team dialogue.
Trauma-Sensitive Case Study Applied New Authority (Haim Omer) principles to address behavioral masking in SEN students. Focused on creating a consistent, safe environment through "relational trust" across the entire teaching team.
Socratic Practice Utilizing "The unexamined life is not worth living" in a scientific context. Testing our teaching methods with the same rigor we apply to laboratory results.
